Money in the Market

At first glance the positive/negative signs in this figure may be a bit confusing. Think of it as representing the net change of money the politician has put into/pulled of a stock. So if a politician were to buy $100 of stock ZZZ then this figure would read ZZZ -100. If they were then to sell $50 of ZZZ then the figure would read ZZZ $50. This figure is a great way to tell if a politician has bought or sold more money from a stock. This can also be thought of as simply summing the exchanges from the Trading Info figure.

Gains If Stocks Close

This figure shows how much money your politician would have if they sold the remainder of their shares today (4/23/2024). Feel free to email me if you want this figure updated. This figure is a great way to tell how well your politician is doing currently in the stocks they have invested in.

Confidence Using NPC

NPC stands for Normalized Politician Confidence. This is the change in buying and selling over the total amount of money a politician has in the market. This means that a negative NPC index represents a politician who has sold more of an asset than they have purchased. This figure is great to have an internal metric of how your politician ranks the assets in their portfolio.

Confidence Using SPC

SPC stands for Scaled Politician Confidence. This is the ratio between the amount of money a politician has purchased of a share divided by how much money they have sold of that share (plus one). This is just another metric you can use to see how your politicians think about their portfolio. I am always looking for better ways to represent confidence, if you have any ideas email me!

Money Made Against Confidence (SPC)

These last few graphs are the respective confidence indexes against how much money your politician has made in the market (if all open holdings are closed). Additionally, there is a correlation between how much money they have made and how confident they are in the stocks they have purchased. A high correlation means that if you see this politician make a big (with respect to their portfolio) buy then there's a good chance that stock will do well!
